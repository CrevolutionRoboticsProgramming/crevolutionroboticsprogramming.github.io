Little Astronauts Space Camp

Discover Space and Science Through Hands-On STEM Exploration

Campers become junior scientists and engineers as they explore the Sun, planets, and space through exciting, hands-on STEM activities. Students investigate invisible ultraviolet (UV) light using color-changing beads, design their own Mars bases to support human life, and conduct crater experiments to understand how impacts shape the Moon.

Through guided experiments and creative challenges, campers learn how scientists ask questions, test ideas, and observe results. They explore how sunlight affects our world, how astronauts might survive on Mars, and how craters form on planetary surfaces.

This program introduces young learners to the foundations of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) in a fun and engaging environment that builds curiosity, confidence, teamwork, and problem-solving skills.
